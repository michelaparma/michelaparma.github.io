Who am I?

I am Michela Parma, student of Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge at the University of Bologna.

Creativity, curiosity and interest in design have led me to this creative and communication medium that is the web. My course of study has changed a lot over the years. I have gone from love for architecture to love for History. I have never denied either of them because both have allowed me to understand what I really like to do: create websites and enrich them with content. On the one hand the sensitivity for design, color matching and the choice of images, and on the other hand the skills of historical research.
I proudly call myself Brutalist lover. Brutalism is a design current that applies to both architecture and web development : geometric, rough and visually heavy elements that are reflected in my creative choices

What I love about web design

I like to see how a project comes to life, from the original idea to the design on paper and finally to the technological implementation. I like to watch the project from start to finish, and have people test the finished product. I like to study human behavior in relation to computers, the conscious and subconscious choices of people using a tool and the needs of users. Finally, I like the infinite possibilities offered by digital support.

Other interests

I like singing, creating tunes, acting, baking cakes and I love chihuahuas.

Project developed for the course of Knowledge Organization and Digital Methods in the Cultural Heritage Domain (University of Bologna) in collaboration with my team. For the 60th anniversary of the release of the movie "La Dolce Vita", a Link Open Data model has been implemented. It focuses on the production and distribution of this cult movie directed by Federico Fellini in 1960.

Available at div-o.github.io/

Project developed for the course of Knowledge Representation and Extraction (University of Bologna) in collaboration with my team. DIV-O stands for Dante Inferno V - Ontology. The project presents a tool to explore the Canto V through four differet editions of Divina commedia, according to characters and places, both real and fictional, and literary references present in the Canto. It's also possible to analise the relations identified in the Canto V through the Knowledge Graph.
